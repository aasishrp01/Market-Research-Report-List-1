<p><p>Signaling System 7 (SS7) is a telecommunication protocol used to control and manage communication networks. Its market is categorized into several types:</p><p>1. Access Link: Enables communication between a subscriber and the network, allowing access to services.</p><p>2. Bridge Link: Connects two different signaling points or networks, providing interoperability.</p><p>3. Cross Link: Connects signaling points within the same network, ensuring signaling exchange.</p><p>4. Diagonal Link: Connects non-adjacent signaling points within a network, providing alternative paths.</p><p>5. Extended Link: Supports communication over long distances by extending the signaling links.</p><p>6. Fully Associated Link: Allows signaling points to exchange information without restrictions, ensuring complete interoperability.</p></p>
