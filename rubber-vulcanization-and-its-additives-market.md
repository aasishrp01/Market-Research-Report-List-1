<p><p>Rubber vulcanization is a chemical process used to convert raw rubber into a more durable and stable material by cross-linking the polymer chains. This process involves the addition of various chemicals known as vulcanization additives or accelerators, which enhance the performance and properties of rubber products.</p><p>Some commonly used rubber vulcanization additives include sulfur, accelerators (such as mercaptobenzothiazole and benzothiazyl disulfide), activators, and antioxidants. Sulfur is the most widely used vulcanization agent as it forms strong cross-links between rubber chains, improving the mechanical strength and resistance to heat, aging, and deformation.</p><p>The global market for rubber vulcanization and its additives is expected to witness significant growth in the coming years. The increasing demand for rubber products in various industries like automotive, construction, and electronics is the major driving factor for this growth. Additionally, the growing focus on developing eco-friendly and sustainable rubber products is creating opportunities for new types of vulcanization additives.</p><p>The surge in infrastructure projects, expanding automotive industry, and rising technological advancements are boosting the demand for rubber vulcanization and its additives.
