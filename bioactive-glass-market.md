<p><p>Bioactive glass is a type of material that possesses both regenerative and therapeutic properties, making it widely applicable in healthcare and biotechnology industries. It is composed of various bioactive elements like calcium, silica, sodium, and phosphorus, which enable it to bond with living tissues. When bioactive glass comes into contact with body fluids, it forms a hydroxycarbonate apatite layer on its surface, stimulating the growth of bone tissue and facilitating the healing process.</p><p>The market analysis of the bioactive glass industry indicates that it is poised for steady growth in the coming years. The growing prevalence of bone-related disorders, such as osteoporosis and osteoarthritis, is anticipated to drive the demand for bioactive glass in orthopedic applications. Additionally, the rising adoption of minimally invasive surgeries and increasing geriatric population further fuel the market growth.</p><p>Moreover, advancements in material science and increased research and development activities have led to the development of bioactive glass with enhanced properties, such as high mechanical strength and controlled biodegradability. These factors are likely to boost the market in the forecast period.</p><p>The rising trend of dental implants and dental bone grafting procedures is also expected to contribute to the growth of the bioactive glass market.
