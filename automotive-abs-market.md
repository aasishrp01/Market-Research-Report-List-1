<p><p>Automotive ABS (Anti-lock Braking System) is a safety feature in vehicles that prevents the wheels from locking up during braking. The market for ABS systems can be categorized into three types: One-channel Type, Three-channel Type, and Four-channel Type. </p><p>The One-channel Type is a basic ABS system that controls only one wheel, usually the rear wheel. The Three-channel Type controls three wheels, typically the two front wheels and one rear wheel. The Four-channel Type is the most advanced and efficient, controlling all four wheels independently. These different types cater to varying vehicle requirements and provide varying degrees of braking precision and stability.</p></p>
